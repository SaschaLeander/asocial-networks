Asocial Media

The premise of our critique is that media platforms do not improve the well-being of the people who use them.

Instead, their design ruthlessly maximises the time people spend on the platform consuming uninformative content to increase a company's profit. This is achieved by algorithms whose loss function corresponds to the inverse of the time users spend on the platform.

Our project's aim is twofold. We want to:

analyse the system in which media platforms are embedded and justified, in particular, so-called social media as a specific type of alienation in our capitalist society

and

explore the mechanisms that make people consume uninformative content, and how to counter this behaviour.

We are students who got to know each other through the Studienkolleg on “Signal & Noise” 2025/26.
